# Visual Direction

## Tone
The game should feel like:
- a magical tournament trial,
- an elegant but dangerous puzzle experience,
- a stylized hedge-maze challenge at night.

## Keywords
- dark
- enchanted
- refined
- tense
- mossy
- gilded
- misty

## Palette Direction
Primary suggestions:
- deep green
- dark desaturated teal
- warm gold
- parchment beige
- stone gray
- muted moonlit highlights

Avoid:
- neon colors,
- overly saturated cartoon palettes,
- high-noise textures that reduce readability.

## Environment Style
The maze should suggest:
- tall hedges,
- magical darkness,
- drifting mist,
- carved markers,
- distant glow around the Cup.

Use visual framing to direct attention:
- brighter clue panel,
- bright finish target,
- subtle spotlighting around important choices.

## UI Motifs
Preferred motifs:
- engraved stone panels
- parchment cards
- metallic trim
- magical glyphs
- soft glow accents

## Typography Direction
Use readable fantasy-leaning type choices, but do not sacrifice clarity.
Title can be more decorative.
Body and clue text should remain highly legible.

## Animation Direction
Animations should support responsiveness, not delay it.

Good uses:
- clue reveal shimmer
- key unlock pulse
- mist drift
- wrong-path hedge movement
- finish glow

Avoid:
- long unskippable flourishes,
- excessive particle spam,
- effects that obscure clue text.

## Asset Strategy
Early prototype can use:
- geometric placeholders,
- simple textures,
- procedural shapes,
- public-domain or original temporary art.

Later passes can introduce:
- custom icons,
- decorative frames,
- better environmental sprites,
- audio polish.

## Thematic Asset Ideas
- golden egg
- rune discs
- enchanted stone markers
- Triwizard Cup silhouette
- hedge thorns
- sparks or drifting magical motes
